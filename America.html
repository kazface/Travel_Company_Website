American Dream

Chicago to LA tour

Duration: 11 days and 10 nights Tour Type: USA Escorted Coach Tours Tour Activity: Discover America Tour Region: , Across the USA , American Southwest , California and West Coast , Chicago and Great Lakes , Midwest , Rocky Mountains Region

Day 1 in Chicago

Today, we'll take a quick tour of the city's top attractions, including Lake Shore Drive, the Magnificent Mile, and Navy Pier. The Willis Tower (entry not included), one of the world's tallest structures, provides a striking backdrop for this cultural and financial center in the Windy City's magnificent high rise architecture. The remainder of the day is yours to do as you choose.

Day 2 : Chicago – Crossing the Mississippi River – Sioux Falls

Travel through Midwest farm area, which is rich in maize, wheat, and dairy farms. Then we cross the Mississippi River and proceed into Iowa, arriving on the Missouri River's banks 200 years ago, exactly as Lewis and Clark did. Sioux Falls is where you'll spend the night.

Day 3 : Sioux Falls – Badlands National Park – Mt. Rushmore National Memorial – Deadwood/Rapid City

Travel west across South Dakota's plains to Badlands National Park, which is home to strangely formed and colored rock formations and was formerly home to buffalo and antelope. After that, we'll travel to the Black Hills, which are holy to Plains Indians and home to Mount Rushmore.

Day 4: Deadwood/Rapid City – Bighorn Mountains – Cody

Enter the “Equality State,” Wyoming, which has twelve mountain ranges intermixed with deserts and rolling grasslands, and continue to Cody for the night.

Day 5-6: Cody – Yellowstone National Park – Grand Teton National Park – Salt Lake City

Take a scenic drive through Wyoming's mountains to Yellowstone National Park, America's first national park, and take a sightseeing tour of this vast geothermal wonderland, which includes geysers, waterfalls, wildlife, and canyons.Day 6 Cody – Yellowstone National Park – Grand Teton National Park – Salt Lake City Take a scenic drive through Wyoming's mountains to Yellowstone National Park, America's first national park, and take a sightseeing tour of this vast geothermal wonderland, which includes geysers, waterfalls, wildlife, and canyons.

Day 7 : Bryce Canyon – Lake Powell – Grand Canyon – Flagstaff

This morning, depart Bryce Canyon for Lake Powell, where crystal blue water laps against towering red-rock canyon walls. Lake Powell is America's second-largest man-made lake, and one of a series of lakes created by damming the Colorado River.Travel through Grand Teton National Park (weather allowed) to view breathtaking mountain peaks that rise to nearly 13,000 feet (4,000 meters). Arrive at Utah, the Mormon state, and enjoy a short city tour of Salt Lake City, the state capital.

Day 8: Route 66 — Flagstaff/Seligman – Las Vegas

Depart Flagstaff this morning for Las Vegas, the glittering "Entertainment Capital of the World." Pass through Seligman, a town constructed along Route 66 that seems as if it has been stuck in time for the past sixty years. The rest of the day is yours to explore the world-famous Las Vegas strip.
